Overview

This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of mortgage pipeline performance across branches. It highlights funded volume, fallout behavior, borrower characteristics, and branch level trends. The objective is to understand how market conditions, borrower profiles, and operational execution shape overall production.

Key Insights
  • Los Angeles leads in funded volume. This is driven largely by higher property values rather than purely superior operational performance.
  • Charlotte performs strongly relative to its market. It produces competitive funded volume in a more modest housing environment, suggesting operational efficiency.
  • Chicago shows the highest suspension rate. Suspended loans are often recovered, indicating effective loan officer engagement.
  • Charlotte has the highest fallout rate. Borrowers tend to cancel outright rather than enter suspended status.
  • Retail outperforms wholesale. Retail loans show stronger borrower profiles and lower fallout.
  • Atlanta displays the strongest borrower profiles. Higher loan amounts, lower leverage, and stronger credit quality define this branch.
  • Withdrawals cluster in mid tier FICO ranges. This challenges the assumption that weaker credit drives fallout.
  • LTV influences fallout more than FICO. The highest fallout occurs in the eighty five to ninety percent LTV band.
  • Funding timelines are consistent across branches. This suggests standardized fulfillment processes.
  • Branch performance is tightly grouped. Closed rates vary only slightly, indicating strong process control.
Narrative Analysis

Los Angeles produces the largest funded volume in the network. This aligns with expectations given the higher property values in that market. When adjusting for market size, Charlotte emerges as a standout performer. It generates competitive funded volume in a more modest housing environment, which suggests that its results are driven by operational efficiency rather than market inflation.

Chicago records the highest suspension rate. This pattern can indicate friction in documentation or underwriting, yet the branch does not show a corresponding spike in withdrawals. Instead, loans are frequently suspended and then recovered. This suggests that loan officers and operations teams are actively working suspended files and successfully moving them toward closing.

Charlotte shows the opposite behavior. It has the highest fallout rate while maintaining a lower suspension rate. Borrowers in this market appear more likely to cancel outright rather than enter suspended status. This may reflect borrower sensitivity to rate movement or competitive pressure.

Retail production outperforms wholesale in both funded volume and borrower quality. Retail loans show higher average loan amounts, lower leverage, lower DTI, and stronger FICO scores. Wholesale loans exhibit higher fallout, which may reflect less direct borrower engagement or greater pricing sensitivity.

Atlanta displays the strongest borrower characteristics across the network. Its loans show higher average amounts, lower leverage, lower DTI, and higher FICO scores. This suggests a healthier borrower base and lower long term credit risk.

Withdrawn loans cluster in the middle of the FICO distribution rather than at the lowest end. Borrowers in the mid tier may be more sensitive to rate changes or qualification uncertainty.

LTV appears to influence withdrawal behavior more than FICO. Loans in the eighty five to ninety percent LTV range show the highest withdrawal percentage, while loans above ninety five percent show the lowest.

Funding timelines remain consistent across branches. This indicates a stable and predictable fulfillment process and strong operational control.

Technical Context

This dashboard is built on a star schema with a central fact table that captures loan pipeline events and dimension tables for borrower attributes, branch information, channel, and loan officer assignments. Measures such as funded rate, fallout rate, suspension rate, and average loan amount are implemented using DAX to ensure accurate filtering across branches and borrower segments.
