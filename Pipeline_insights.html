This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of mortgage pipeline performance across branches. It highlights funded volume, fallout behavior, borrower characteristics, and branch level trends. The objective is to understand how market conditions, borrower profiles, and operational execution shape overall production.

These metrics establish the foundation for understanding production strength, borrower quality, and pipeline health across the organization.

Key Insights

  • Los Angeles leads in funded volume. It produces the largest funded loan amount in the network, driven largely by higher property values rather than purely superior operational performance.
  • Charlotte performs strongly relative to its market. It delivers substantial funded volume in a more modest housing market, which suggests that its results are driven by operational efficiency rather than market inflation.
  • Chicago shows the highest suspension rate. Suspension is elevated, yet withdrawals remain controlled, indicating that loan officers are effective at recovering suspended files and guiding them to resolution.
  • Charlotte has the highest fallout rate. Its suspension rate is lower, which implies that borrowers in this market are more likely to cancel outright rather than enter a suspended status.
  • Retail outperforms wholesale. Retail channels show higher funded volume and stronger borrower profiles, while wholesale exhibits higher fallout and more fragile pipelines.
  • Atlanta displays the strongest borrower profiles. Loans from this branch show higher average amounts, lower leverage, lower DTI, and higher FICO scores, suggesting a healthier borrower base and lower long term credit risk.
  • Withdrawals cluster in mid tier FICO ranges. Withdrawn loans are concentrated between FICO 650 and 750 rather than at the lowest credit bands, challenging the assumption that weaker credit is the primary driver of withdrawals.
  • LTV influences fallout more than FICO. Loans in the 85 to 90 percent LTV band show the highest withdrawal percentage, while loans above 95 percent LTV show the lowest, indicating that leverage and product mix play a larger role than credit score alone.
  • Funding timelines are consistent across branches. Average time from lock to funding is stable, which suggests a standardized and predictable fulfillment process across the network.
  • Branch performance is tightly grouped. Closed rates across branches fall within a narrow range, indicating strong process control and that differences in outcomes are driven more by market conditions and borrower mix than by inconsistent execution.

Narrative Analysis

Los Angeles produces the largest funded volume in the network. This aligns with expectations given the higher property values in that market, which naturally elevate loan amounts. When adjusting for market size and price levels, Charlotte emerges as a standout performer. It generates competitive funded volume in a more modest housing environment, which suggests that its results are driven by operational efficiency rather than market inflation alone.

Chicago records the highest suspension rate. This pattern can indicate friction in documentation or underwriting, yet the branch does not show a corresponding spike in withdrawals. Instead, loans are frequently suspended and then recovered. This behavior suggests that loan officers and operations teams are actively working suspended files and successfully moving them toward closing rather than allowing them to fall out of the pipeline.

Charlotte shows the opposite behavior. It has the highest fallout rate while maintaining a lower suspension rate. Borrowers in this market appear more likely to cancel outright rather than enter a suspended status. This may reflect borrower sensitivity to rate movement, competitive pressure, or local market dynamics where borrowers have more alternatives and are quicker to exit when terms or timing do not align with expectations.

Retail production outperforms wholesale in both funded volume and borrower quality. Retail loans show higher average loan amounts, lower LTV, lower DTI, and stronger FICO scores. Wholesale loans exhibit higher fallout, which may reflect less direct borrower engagement, greater sensitivity to pricing, or more aggressive comparison shopping. This distinction underscores the importance of channel strategy when evaluating pipeline performance and forecasting revenue.

Atlanta displays the strongest borrower characteristics across the network. Its loans show higher average amounts, lower leverage, lower DTI, and higher FICO scores. This combination suggests a healthier borrower base and potentially lower long term credit risk. Although Atlanta does not lead in total volume, its quality profile positions it as a branch with strong risk adjusted value.

Withdrawn loans cluster in the middle of the FICO distribution rather than at the lowest end. A significant share of withdrawals occurs between FICO 650 and 750, which challenges the assumption that weaker credit is the primary driver of fallout. Mid tier borrowers may be more sensitive to rate changes, qualification uncertainty, or competing offers, and may have enough optionality to step away from a transaction when conditions shift.

LTV appears to influence withdrawal behavior more than FICO. Loans in the 85 to 90 percent LTV range show the highest withdrawal percentage, while loans above 95 percent LTV show the lowest. One interpretation is that loans with lower LTV are more often associated with specific programs or first time homebuyer structures that introduce additional complexity and conditions. Borrowers with higher leverage may be more committed to the transaction and less likely to exit once they are in process.

Funding timelines remain consistent across branches. The average time from lock to funding varies only slightly, which indicates a stable and predictable fulfillment process. This consistency is a strength when setting expectations with borrowers and referral partners and suggests that the organization has effective process control in its closing and funding operations.

Overall, branch performance is tightly grouped. Closed rates across branches fall within a narrow band, which indicates that the organization has standardized processes and that differences in outcomes are driven more by market conditions, borrower mix, and channel strategy than by uneven execution. This provides a solid foundation for targeted optimization rather than broad process redesign.

Technical Context

This dashboard is built on a star schema with a central fact table that captures loan pipeline events and dimension tables for borrower attributes, branch information, channel, and loan officer assignments. Measures such as funded rate, fallout rate, suspension rate, and average loan amount are implemented using DAX to ensure accurate filtering across branches, channels, and borrower segments. The model supports interactive filtering by branch, channel, and CLTV group, enabling users to move from high level trends to specific segments without sacrificing performance.
