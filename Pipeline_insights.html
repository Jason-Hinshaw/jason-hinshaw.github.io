Overview

Hinshaw Mortgage Group (a fictional mortgage company) has five branches operating across the United States.

Because each branch operates in a distinct market environment, direct comparisons can obscure the underlying factors that influence performance.

This dashboard was created to reduce that noise by focusing on branch level as well as company wide pipeline performance. This dashboard highlights funded volume, fallout behavior, borrower characteristics, and branch trends to reveal the underlying patterns that drive production, fallout and suspense.

The goal of this dashboard is to help stakeholders understand the factors that influence production within individual branches as well as the pipeline as a whole.

  • Los Angeles leads in funded volume. LA funded $1.15B, the highest of all branches, driven primarily by higher home values rather than operational outperformance.
  • Charlotte performs strongly relative to its market. Despite operating in a lower‑cost market, Charlotte funded $748M with a 74.46% close rate, matching higher‑value markets in efficiency.
  • Chicago shows the highest suspension rate. Chicago posts the highest suspended‑loan share at ~5.6%, with many ultimately converting to funded loans. This speaks to strong conflict resolution amongst LO's at this branch.
  • Charlotte has the highest fallout rate. Charlotte’s fallout rate reaches 19.94%, the highest among branches, with borrowers more likely to cancel outright than enter suspension.
  • Retail outperforms wholesale. Retail loans show stronger borrower profiles (average FICO ~719) and lower fallout compared to wholesale.
  • Atlanta displays the strongest borrower profiles. Atlanta borrowers show the lowest leverage (average LTV ~79%) and strong credit quality.
  • Withdrawals cluster in mid‑tier FICO ranges. 73% of withdrawn loans fall between 651–755 FICO, challenging the assumption that only low‑FICO borrowers withdraw.
  • LTV influences fallout more than FICO. The 85–90% LTV band shows the highest fallout rate, indicating leverage is a stronger predictor of cancellation than credit score.
  • Funding timelines are consistent across branches. Average lock‑to‑funding time holds steady at ~38 days across all markets, reflecting standardized fulfillment processes.
  • Branch performance is tightly grouped. Close rates range narrowly from 73.36% to 74.46%, showing strong process control and minimal operational variance.
  • Conventional Products have the highest funded volume. Conventional products were responsible for roughly 55% of all funded volume.
    • Narrative Analysis

    Los Angeles produces the largest funded volume in the network. This aligns with expectations given the higher property values in that market. When adjusting for market size, Charlotte emerges as a standout performer. It generates competitive funded volume in a more modest housing environment, which suggests that its results are driven by operational efficiency rather than market inflation.

    Chicago records the highest suspension rate. This pattern can indicate friction in documentation or underwriting, yet the branch does not show a corresponding spike in withdrawals. Instead, loans are frequently suspended and then recovered. This suggests that loan officers and operations teams are actively working suspended files and successfully moving them toward closing.

    Charlotte shows the opposite behavior. It has the highest fallout rate while maintaining a lower suspension rate. Borrowers in this market appear more likely to cancel outright rather than enter suspended status. This may reflect borrower sensitivity to rate movement or competitive pressure.

    Retail production outperforms wholesale in both funded volume and borrower quality. Retail loans show higher average loan amounts, lower leverage, lower DTI, and stronger FICO scores. Wholesale loans exhibit higher fallout, which may reflect less direct borrower engagement or greater pricing sensitivity.

    Atlanta displays the strongest borrower characteristics across the network. Its loans show higher average amounts, lower leverage, lower DTI, and higher FICO scores. This suggests a healthier borrower base and lower long term credit risk.

    Withdrawn loans cluster in the middle of the FICO distribution rather than at the lowest end. Borrowers in the mid tier may be more sensitive to rate changes or qualification uncertainty.

    LTV appears to influence withdrawal behavior more than FICO. Loans in the eighty five to ninety percent LTV range show the highest withdrawal percentage, while loans above ninety five percent show the lowest.

    Funding timelines remain consistent across branches. This indicates a stable and predictable fulfillment process and strong operational control.

    Technical Context

    This dashboard is built on a star schema with a central fact table that captures loan pipeline events and dimension tables for borrower attributes, branch information, channel, and loan officer assignments. Measures such as funded rate, fallout rate, suspension rate, and average loan amount are implemented using DAX to ensure accurate filtering across branches and borrower segments.
